# Conv Decisions - 2026-07-19

## 1. Code-repo branch allowlist for all timecard surfaces
**Type:** Implementation
**Topics:** billing, tooling, cc-workflow

**Trigger:** Measured contamination of our Conv 371 timecard bucket by 6 client commits.

**Options Considered:**
1. Strip conv numbers from his commits (rewrite history)
2. Suffix his conv numbers with "b" (rewrite history)
3. Squash his work into one no-history commit
4. Author-based exclusion in timecard config
5. Branch allowlist `^jfg-dev` on the code repo ← Chosen (user directive)

**Decision:** All timecard surfaces ignore any code-repo branch not matching `^jfg-dev`. Config key `rTimecardDay.codeBranchAllowPattern`; enforced at `discoverCandidateBranches()` in `timecard-day.js`, `--branches='jfg-dev*'` in `/r-timecard`, HEAD pre-flight in `/w-timecard`. Docs repo deliberately unfiltered.

**Rationale:** Options 1–2 require force-pushing a branch the client is actively working on, and "Conv 371b:" would fail the conv regex and land in the ad-hoc pool. An allowlist survives new client branches appearing unannounced; a denylist does not.

**Consequences:** Historical timecards can be regenerated cleanly (none sent to client yet). The allowlist **dies on a history-preserving merge**, which makes squash a structural requirement rather than a preference.

> **Insight:** Billing tooling that reads commit metadata inherits every assumption about who writes commits. The moment a repo gains a second author with their own tooling conventions, "read the log" stops being a neutral measurement and becomes a claim that needs a scope.

## 2. Integrate the client's work as squashed commits, not a history-preserving merge
**Type:** Strategic
**Topics:** merge, billing, client-collaboration

**Trigger:** User wanted his conv numbering isolated and his commit history kept out of `jfg-dev*`.

**Options Considered:**
1. Regular merge preserving his 53 commits
2. Squash — his changes land as commits authored by us ← Chosen

**Decision:** His work enters `jfg-dev*` as squashed commits authored by us, with our conv numbering; `brian-July-7` is retained permanently for archaeology.

**Rationale:** Beyond isolating conv numbers, squashing is what keeps Decision 1 working — once his commits sit on a `jfg-dev*` branch, no branch filter can see them.

**Consequences:** `--squash` records no merge parent, so the client must **abandon `brian-July-7` after handoff** and start from the branch we deliver; otherwise every subsequent squash re-presents old changes as conflicts.
