# Conv Learnings - 2026-06-11

## 1. Discovery Rails blob is the natural card source for the home marketing feed
**Topics:** smart-feed, discovery-rails

**Context:** Phase-1 needed marketing cards (trending/popular/new courses & communities).

**Learning:** The Conv-261 `DiscoveryRailsBlob` already carries exactly those 6 rails with `reason`, `topicIds`, `score`, and member counts — so `getMarketingCandidates` only needed a net-new sample-POST query; cards are a pure mapping. Reusing it avoids the duplicate trending/popular/new logic that `[RECO-UNIFY]` #31 is meant to remove.

## 2. The old smart-feed cursor had a latent skip/dupe bug
**Topics:** smart-feed, pagination

**Context:** Implementing Option-A `(created_at,id)` pagination.

**Learning:** The pre-existing cursor used `interleaved.filter(!isDiscovery).pop().activity.time` — the *last interleaved* member post, which after score-sorting is NOT the chronologically-oldest. `created_at < cursor` could then skip or dupe posts between the true min and that value. Option A fixes it: the cursor is the oldest `(created_at,id)` backbone post in the page (the true floor), so the next page is strictly older regardless of in-page score order.

**Pattern:** For score-ordered feeds paginated by a time cursor, derive the cursor from `min(created_at,id)` over the page's backbone items, not from render order.

---

## 3. Replacing a candidate source silently drops its side-filters
**Topics:** smart-feed

**Context:** Swapping `getDiscoveryCandidates` → `getMarketingCandidates` in the orchestrator.

**Learning:** `getDiscoveryCandidates` honored `smart_feed_dismissals`; the replacement didn't, so the swap would have silently regressed the "not interested" feature. ESLint's unused-var on the now-orphaned `dismissedFeeds` was the tell. When replacing a data source, enumerate every filter/behavior the old one applied before deleting its call.
