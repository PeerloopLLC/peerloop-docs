# Conv Decisions - 2026-06-30

## 1. Keep the recall index in MEMORY.md with HOT/COLD tiering (not relocate to CLAUDE.md)
**Type:** Strategic
**Topics:** cc-workflow, docs-infra, memory

**Trigger:** MEMORY.md at 86% of the 25 KB SessionStart auto-load cap, rising ~1 conv at a time; /r-prune-memory lever exhausted.

**Options Considered:**
1. Tiered grammar + archive pass, inside MEMORY.md ← **Chosen** (architecture); archive found nothing to move
2. Full move of the index to uncapped CLAUDE.md (or @import'd file)
3. Hybrid: promote always-on rules to CLAUDE.md, keep situational long-tail in MEMORY.md
4. Keep in MEMORY.md (HOT/COLD) — selected after the user established the index could live in CLAUDE.md

**Decision:** Keep the index in MEMORY.md as a two-tier structure: 🔥 HOT (always-on rules, full marker-rich lines, placed first so they're never truncated) + 📇 COLD (situational long-tail, terse one-liners preserving marker/[CODE]/trigger). New memories default to COLD; promote to HOT deliberately.

**Rationale:** MEMORY.md is framed as "background context"; situational recall pointers are genuinely background, not instructions — relocating them to CLAUDE.md would mis-frame them and dilute adherence to the real rules. Tiering bounds growth (per-entry cost ~halved going forward) and HOT-first ordering structurally protects always-on rules. User confirmed this direction after pressure-testing the CLAUDE.md alternative.

**Consequences:** Live MEMORY.md rewritten 86%→71% (18,066 B), 82 entries intact, 0 broken links. CLAUDE.md gained a §Memory Index Tiering section recording the default-COLD write-time rule. Phase 2 (rewrite /r-prune-memory to enforce + auto-re-tier the grammar) deferred to next conv.
