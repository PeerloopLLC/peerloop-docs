# Conv Learnings - 2026-06-14

## 1. Byte-trimming a dense MEMORY.md has a hard floor; bundling beats shaving
**Topics:** memory, cc-workflow

**Context:** [MEM-CAP] — 18 re-flatten/extract edits only moved MEMORY.md 89%→83% bytes, far short of the projected 75%.

**Learning:** Once the Conv-213 filename-echo bloat is gone and detail already lives in sub-files, MEMORY.md sits at a ~200B/line "balanced density" floor enforced by the load-bearing-marker rule. Trimming trades retrievability for bytes at ~75-190B/line. The high-leverage lever is **retiring/bundling whole index lines** (coherence-check), not shaving. The PLATO fold alone (4 lines, pure duplicates) beat the entire 18-line trim pass.

## 2. Load-on-demand bundles must keep safety headlines in the always-loaded index
**Topics:** memory, cc-workflow

**Context:** Folding `feedback_never_modify_figma` (a guardrail) behind a `figma-context.md` trigger.

**Learning:** A safety/guardrail rule can move behind a "Load when X" trigger ONLY because its risk exists exclusively during X-work (when the trigger fires) — but the trigger's index line must still carry the guardrail headline inline ("Figma is READ-ONLY — NEVER call write-shaped tools"), so the rule stays salient even when the detail isn't loaded.

## 3. `plato-context.md` was already a superset of its 4 satellite entries
**Topics:** memory

**Context:** Planning the PLATO fold.

**Learning:** Pre-existing "context" bundle files can silently accumulate full copies of sibling rules' content. Always diff the bundle against the satellites before migrating — here the fold was pure deletion (zero content movement), which is the cheapest possible consolidation.
