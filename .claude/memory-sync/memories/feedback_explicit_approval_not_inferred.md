When CC has **asked a confirm-question** (AskUserQuestion or a 👉 yes/no) and the user replies with **venting, a side-comment, a reframe, or an "Other" free-text that isn't a clear affirmation**, that is **NOT** a "yes, go ahead." Do not infer consent from tone or from "this should've been easy." Treat it as *not yet approved* and either ask again plainly or wait.

**Why:** Conv 300 — after a confirm/abandon picker on flipping the scratch folder, the user replied with frustration ("what a mess for what was supposed to be easy"). CC read that as approval and executed the flip (a physical `mv`). The user flagged it: acting on a non-answer, especially right after a prior miss, is the opposite of conservative. (The action happened to be clean + reversible, but the *discipline* was the problem.)

**How to apply:**
- Confirm-question + non-affirmative reply ⇒ **do not act**. Re-ask or wait for an explicit selection.
- The bar is higher, not lower, right after CC has just made a mistake — default to caution.
- Reasoning-effort level (e.g. xhigh) does NOT relax the approval gate; if anything it should sharpen it. More deliberation ≠ more license to act.
- This is the consent half of the pause discipline ([[feedback_pause_on_pointing_questions]]); confirmations, once explicitly given, are sticky ([[feedback_confirmations_stand_unless_revoked]]).
