## Decision 1: Vertical Scroll Feed (No Grid)

**Previous State:** Course listings use responsive grid (3-col desktop, 2-col tablet, 1-col mobile) with pagination
**New State:** Single-column vertical scroll feed (like social media) with infinite scroll

### What This Means

All course listings should display one course at a time in a vertical scrolling feed, matching the validated social media UX pattern. No pagination controls, no "Showing X of Y" messaging.

### Requirements

1. **Vertical Display**
   - All course cards displayed in single column
   - No side-by-side cards on any breakpoint
   - Cards scroll vertically like a social feed
   - Interface consistent with social feed patterns

2. **Infinite Scroll**
   - No pagination buttons (remove entirely)
   - No "Showing 1-12 of 48 courses" text (remove entirely)
   - Load more courses as user scrolls
   - Never require user to click to see next page

3. **Affected Pages**
   - HOME (course sections)
   - CBRO (Course Browse - primary change)
   - CPRO (Creator profile courses)
   - CRLS (Creator listing - TBD)

### User Interactions
- **Visitor/Student:** Scrolls through courses continuously
- **No pagination actions required**

### Brian's Rationale
> "The scroll theme has been validated with all social media. It is easier to view one course at a time like a social feed. So courses will scroll just like feeds so we keep the interface consistent."

## Decision 3: Revenue Split Clarification

**Previous State:** "Earn 85% of course sales" (ambiguous)
**New State:** Creators earn 85% only when they personally teach

### What This Means

The 85% creator commission applies specifically when the creator teaches their own course directly. When Student-Teachers teach, the creator earns 15% royalty. The primary income for most creators will be passive 15% royalties from the Student-Teacher network.

### Revenue Model Clarification

| Scenario | Creator | Student-Teacher | Platform |
|----------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| Creator teaches own course | 85% | - | 15% |
| S-T teaches creator's course | 15% | 70% | 15% |

### Required Updates

1. **FCRE Page**
   - Change "Earn 85% of course sales" to "Earn 85% when you teach your course"
   - Clarify 15% royalty on S-T sessions
   - Emphasize passive income from S-T network

2. **PRIC Page**
   - Update earnings calculator to reflect dual model
   - Show 85% teaching vs 15% royalty scenarios

3. **HOWI Page**
   - Clarify Step 2: "you keep 85%" = when you teach
   - Step 4: "15% of every tutoring session" = S-T sessions

### Brian's Quote
> "Also, creators will only make 85% on courses they teach. Otherwise it is 15% which will be most of a creators income."
> "This is partial info for the creator. He only keeps 85% when he teaches his course."

## Decision 4: Terminology - "Student-Teacher" Not "Tutor"

**Previous State:** Mixed use of "tutor" and "Student-Teacher"
**New State:** Use "Student-Teacher" exclusively

### What This Means

The term "tutor" implies a top-level expert, which doesn't match the peer teaching model. "Student-Teacher" better describes graduates who teach while continuing to learn.

### Requirements

1. **Global Search & Replace**
   - Replace "tutor" with "Student-Teacher" (or "S-T" in technical docs)
   - Replace "tutoring" with "peer teaching" or "1-on-1 teaching"

2. **SEO Impact**
   - Old targets: "peer tutoring pricing", "affordable tutoring"
   - New targets: TBD - need to research alternatives
   - Possible: "peer teaching", "affordable 1-on-1 teaching", "learn from peers"

3. **Affected Areas**
   - All marketing pages (FCRE, PRIC, HOWI, BTAT)
   - User stories
   - Documentation
   - UI copy

### Brian's Quote
> "I am thinking we need to stay away from the word tutor. That implies a top level expert. For now I prefer student-teacher."

## Decision 5: Community Time Investment

**Previous State:** Heavy emphasis on "passive income" for creators
**New State:** Acknowledge community management takes creator time

### What This Means

While S-T royalties are passive income, managing course communities requires active creator involvement. Marketing copy should be realistic about time commitment.

### Requirements

1. **FCRE Page**

   - Mention moderator assignment as a tool
   - Be honest about time investment

### Brian's Quote
